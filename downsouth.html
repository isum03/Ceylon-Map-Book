1. Galle Fort

Galle Fort was initially built by the Portuguese in the late 16th century, then extensively fortified by the Dutch during the 17th century, and later modified by the British in the 19th century. Its strategic location on the southwestern coast of Sri Lanka made it a crucial hub for trade between Asia and Europe, particularly during the colonial era.

2. Mirissa Beach

Mirissa Beach, located on the southern coast of Sri Lanka, is renowned for its stunning beauty, relaxed atmosphere, and opportunities for whale watching.
Mirissa Beach is characterized by its pristine golden sand, turquoise waters, and palm-fringed shoreline. The beach stretches in a gentle curve, creating a picturesque setting thatâ€™s ideal for sunbathing, swimming, and leisurely walks along the shore.

3. Unawatuna Beach

Unawatuna Beach, located near Galle on the southern coast of Sri Lanka, is renowned for its stunning beauty, clear turquoise waters, and relaxed atmosphere.
Unawatuna Beach is a crescent-shaped bay fringed with palm trees and soft golden sand. The calm, shallow waters make it ideal for swimming and snorkeling, especially around the coral reefs just offshore. The beach is sheltered by headlands on either side, creating a tranquil environment perfect for relaxation.

4. Hikkaduwa Beach

Hikkaduwa Beach, situated along the southwest coast of Sri Lanka, is renowned for its vibrant atmosphere, diverse marine life, and excellent opportunities for water sports.
Hikkaduwa Beach stretches along a wide expanse of golden sand framed by swaying palm trees and the gentle lapping waves of the Indian Ocean. The beach itself is picturesque, with clear turquoise waters ideal for swimming and snorkeling.

5. Dondra Lighthouse

Dondra Lighthouse, located at Dondra Head on the southernmost tip of Sri Lanka, is a majestic landmark with historical significance and breathtaking panoramic views of the Indian Ocean.
Dondra Lighthouse, also known as Point Dondra Lighthouse, is Sri Lanka's tallest lighthouse, standing at an impressive height of 49 meters (161 feet). It was constructed by the British in 1889 during the colonial era to guide ships navigating the treacherous waters around Dondra Point, which marks the southern extremity of the island.
